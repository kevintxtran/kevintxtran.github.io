National Football League

PRODUCT DESIGN INTERNSHIP | SUMMER 2019

Introduction

This past summer I worked as a Product Design Intern on the UX/Product Design Team at the National Football League. My two biggest projects were creating a quickstart guide for the design system and increasing engagement with the Insights page. In addition, I worked across several smaller projects applying acquired user research.

Overview

I had the unique opportunity to learn about the shift of user goals from the off-season to the pre-season through user research. I attended the training camps of the LA Chargers, LA Rams, and Dallas Cowboys to conduct ethnographic studies and interview fans.

To complement this qualitative user research, I worked with the UX team to handle results from SUPR-Q tests and transform data in Excel and Tableau to turn the interview responses into tangible quantitative data. Taking this data-driven approach allows the NFL be able to present the results of their products/services across multiple platforms, making it plausible to have a visual numerical representation of how successful their changes are to track their growth.

I also constructed use cases for a possible floating action button (FAB) on the Insights page. My goal was to understand what kind of insights users would be likely to read based on the context of: level of understanding, team stats, and player stats.

My work centered around the Insights page

Takeaways

Our team emphasized understanding the fan. Football can be a massive part of someone’s life, but it can be a time killer for another. Our primary focus was to understand the different kinds of fans within the football space, and be able to design the insights page to cater towards all levels of fanatics.

Working for the NFL prior to its centennial season was a rollercoaster. Thanks for letting this Chargers fanboy experience a once in a lifetime opportunity.
