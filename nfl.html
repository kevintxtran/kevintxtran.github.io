National Football League

PRODUCT DESIGN INTERNSHIP | SUMMER 2019

Introduction

This past summer I worked as a Product Design Intern on the UX and Product Design Team at the National Football League. My two biggest projects were ensuring the scalability of the design system and increasing engagement with the Insights page.

Overview

Designing for football is unique because user needs change seasonally. I had the unique opportunity to learn about the shift of user goals from the off-season to the pre-season. I was fortunate enough to attend the training camps of the Dallas Cowboys, LA Rams, and LA Chargers to conduct ethnographic user research. This research served as the groundwork for other projects.

To complement qualitative user research, the NFL is beginning to implement quantitative user research. I worked with the UX team to handle data from SUPR-Q and transform that data into a presentable form. The goal was to take a data-driven approach to the usability of NFL products across multiple platforms.

I also constructed use cases for a possible floating action button (FAB) on the Insights page and was a part of the team that is going to revamp the Insights page. The goal of the team is to understand what kind of insights fans would like to see based on the context of multiple factors including level of understanding, team stats, and player stats.

My work centered around the Insights page

Takeaways

Our team emphasized understanding the fan. Football can be a massive part of someone’s life, but it can be a time killer for another. I needed to hammer into my head the different kinds of people who watch football in order to become a better designer. Context and fan understanding gave me credibility as a collaborator.

User understanding sets the foundation for the rest of the design process and fosters communication across multiple teams. I felt more confident in all of my work and my feedback.

My biggest challenge was wrapping my head around designing for such a large fanbase. Football is the #1 sport in America. There are always trade-offs. With these trade-offs comes the need to iterate. With iteration, comes the need to support the new iteration with data. I’ve come to learn the importance of tangible data through quantitative user research and how it’s necessary to support business goals. The design process is evolving, and I couldn’t be more excited for the challenges that it may pose.

Working for the NFL prior to its centennial season was a rollercoaster. Thanks for letting this Chargers fanboy experience a once in a lifetime opportunity
