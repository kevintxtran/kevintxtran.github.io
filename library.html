UC San Diego Library

UX DESIGN WORK | SEP 2018 - JUN 2019

Overview

I was brought on as UX Assistant to help synthesize user research about students’ interactions with help channels and design assets for a visual update.


Outcomes

  1. A 15% increase in overall engagement with help channels
  2. A visual update with proper calls to action
  3. Overwhelmingly positive feedback from faculty/students on the real-time visualization

1. Help Channels

Students were unaware certain online help channels even existed, so my team asked how we could increase engagement with these channels. I conducted interviews and created heatmaps to gain a better understanding of why students didn't seek out a librarian's help.

  • Students didn't know librarians are experts in information tech and can help beyond bookkeeping.
  • An overwhelmingly majority of students acknowledge the library have resources like computer labs, but never had a reason to speak to a librarian.
  • When analyzing heatmaps, students didn't even bother to hover over help channels. It begged the question of whether or students couldn't see the help channels or they willfully ignored it.

With that in mind, I wanted my prototypes to focus on visibility and changing students' mental models of librarians.

I accomplished the library's goals by creating:

  • A hero cover that briefly communicates that librarians can help you with anything.
  • Easily distinguishable help channel sections with proper iconography.
  • A reevaluation of information architecture; separation between quick help and advanced help.

The re-imagined "ask us" page

2. Asset Creation

Along with reimagining help channels, I created buttons/widgets that aligned with the library's visual guidelines and improved overall visibility.

My team wanted to these widgets to have proper calls to actions. Previously, impressions of a library's website was that it was outdated, static, and used to search for books. I helped create a chat widget and revised wording on some buttons surrounding the Ask Us page.

New widgets, buttons that align with visual guidelines

3. Seating Visualization

I also conducted research to inform the creation of real-time seating visualization, which would help students locate empty seats in the library. My research involved interviews, guerilla testing, and surveys to determine their library related needs/goals during the school year. During exam season, students detested scouring the 8-floor library to find a seat.

My research also drove the creation of the interface of visualization and where it would be placed.

  • Geisel Library is an iconic piece of architecture that is recognizable to all UC San Diego students. Therefore, after experimenting with prototypes, we determined a proper way to visualize library seating was to mimic the library's shape.
