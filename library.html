UC San Diego Library

UX DESIGN WORK | SEP 2018 - JUN 2019

Overview

I was brought on as UX Assistant to help synthesize user research about students’ interactions with help channels and design assets for a visual update.

  1. Synthesize user research to help increase engagement with online help channels
  2. Create assets for the library's visual update
  3. Conduct user research to inform the creation of a real-time seating visualization

Increasing Engagement

Students were unaware certain online help channels even existed, so my team asked how we could increase engagement with these channels. Based off the user research, we re-evaluated the information architecture and removed library jargon.

We wanted to improve visibility and deliver the message that librarians are experts who help you beyond bookkeeping.

The re-imagined "ask us" page


Asset Creation

Along with reimagining help channels, I created buttons/widgets that aligned with the library's visual guidelines and improved overall visibility.

New widgets, buttons that align with visual guidelines


Seating Visualization

I also conducted research to inform the creation of real-time seating visualization. This visualization allowed students to quickly view busy/not busy floors and allow them to find a seat quickly.

My research involved interviews, guerilla testing, and surveys to determine whether or not students would need such a visualization. My research also served as the foundation the interface of visualization and where it would be placed.

Photo courtesy of library.ucsd.edu

Outcomes

  • A 15% increase in overall engagement with help channels
  • A visual update with proper calls to action
  • Overwhelmingly positive feedback from faculty/students on the real-time visualization

My time at the UC San Diego Library came to an end as did my tenure as a student, but I value my experience greatly. Thank you Jenn and SuHui for teaching me so much!
